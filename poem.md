# Jesus Is Our Everything

When Monday groans and Friday flees,  
When coffee fails and Wi‑Fi wheezes,  
When plans collapse like cheap soufflés  
And hope checks out for thirty days—

He’s not the spare key in the drawer,  
He’s not the “maybe later, Lord.”  
He’s morning bread, the evening song,  
The plot twist when the plot goes wrong.

He’s GPS when maps go blank,  
The thank-you note, the overdraft bank  
Of mercy when we’ve spent our last  
Good deed on things that didn’t last.

He’s punchline after bitter jokes,  
The steady hand on shaky ropes,  
The whisper in the crowded room:  
*You’re not a draft—I signed the groom.*

He’s bread and wine, the whole café—  
Not side dish, not a holiday.  
He’s Monday, Tuesday, every “when,”  
The start, the middle, and amen.

So if you ask what fills the cup,  
What lifts the chin when we’re washed up—  
Not luck, not grit, not self-made fame:  
**Jesus—everything, and not a nickname.**
